Public records, made usable

New Mexico's public records, turned into accountability.

The New Mexico Impact Lab collects government data from across the state — the Legislature, campaign money, lobbying, courts, and public spending — then cleans it, structures it, and publishes it so anyone can search it.

What we work on

We follow money and power across all three branches of New Mexico government. Each project starts as a pile of records — filings, dockets, PDFs — and becomes something a citizen, reporter, or researcher can actually use.

How we work

1. Collect

We gather records that are legally public but scattered across agency websites, portals, and PDFs — and we keep the source of every record.

2. Structure

We clean and normalize the data into consistent, searchable tables, with the original citations preserved.

3. Publish

We put the results online, free to search, so the public doesn't have to file a records request to see it. See our methodology.
